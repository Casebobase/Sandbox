Top five shooting guards in history

1. Michael Jordan

widely regarded as the greatest basketball player of all time, played 15 NBA seasons with the Chicago Bulls and Washington Wizards. He averaged 30.1 points, 6.2 rebounds, and 5.3 assists per game, holding the highest career scoring average in NBA history. A six-time NBA champion and five-time MVP, Jordan won 10 scoring titles and was a 14-time All-Star. He shot 49.7% from the field and totaled 32,292 career points. Known for his clutch performances, he led the Bulls to two three-peats in the 1990s. Jordan's impact transcended basketball, making him a global icon.

2. Kobe Bryant

Kobe Bryant, one of the greatest players in NBA history, played 20 seasons with the Los Angeles Lakers. He averaged 25.0 points, 5.2 rebounds, and 4.7 assists per game, finishing his career with 33,643 total points, ranking among the NBA's all-time leading scorers. A five-time NBA champion and two-time Finals MVP, he was an 18-time All-Star and won the 2008 league MVP award. Bryant also secured two scoring titles and was known for his elite footwork, clutch performances, and relentless work ethic. He shot 44.7% from the field and scored 81 points in a single game, the second-highest in NBA history. His legacy as a competitor and global icon continues to inspire athletes worldwide.

3. Allen Iverson

Allen Iverson, one of the most dynamic and influential guards in NBA history, played 14 seasons, primarily with the Philadelphia 76ers. He averaged 26.7 points, 6.2 assists, and 3.7 rebounds per game, ranking among the league’s top scorers. A four-time scoring champion and 11-time All-Star, he won the 2001 MVP award and led the 76ers to the NBA Finals that year. Iverson totaled 24,368 career points and is widely regarded as one of the greatest pound-for-pound players ever. Known for his signature crossover, fearless scoring ability, and toughness despite his 6-foot frame, he redefined the role of smaller guards in the NBA. His cultural impact, from his playing style to his influence on fashion and hip-hop, continues to inspire generations of athletes and fans.

4. Tracy McGrady

Tracy McGrady, a seven-time All-Star and two-time scoring champion, was one of the most talented scorers of his era. Over his 15-season NBA career, he averaged 19.6 points, 5.6 rebounds, and 4.4 assists per game while playing for teams like the Toronto Raptors, Orlando Magic, and Houston Rockets. His best years came in Orlando and Houston, where he dominated as a versatile scorer, averaging over 30 points per game in the 2002-03 season. McGrady totaled 18,381 career points and was known for his smooth shooting, athleticism, and ability to take over games. Despite injuries affecting the latter part of his career, he left a lasting impact, including a legendary 13 points in 35 seconds comeback performance. In 2017, he was inducted into the Basketball Hall of Fame, solidifying his legacy as one of the greats.

5. Dwayne Wade

Dwyane Wade, one of the greatest shooting guards in NBA history, played 16 seasons, primarily with the Miami Heat. He averaged 22.0 points, 5.4 assists, and 4.7 rebounds per game, finishing his career with 23,165 total points. A three-time NBA champion and 13-time All-Star, Wade was the 2006 Finals MVP after leading Miami to its first championship. Known for his slashing ability, clutch performances, and defensive prowess, he also earned eight All-NBA selections and three All-Defensive Team honors. He was the league’s scoring champion in 2009, averaging 30.2 points per game. Wade’s leadership and impact on the game solidified his place as one of the most influential players of his generation.
