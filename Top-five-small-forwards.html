Top five small forwards in history

1. Lebron James

LeBron James, widely regarded as one of the greatest basketball players in history, has had an illustrious NBA career spanning 22 seasons with the Cleveland Cavaliers, Miami Heat, and Los Angeles Lakers. As of February 2025, he has played in 1,536 regular-season games, averaging 27.0 points, 7.5 rebounds, and 7.4 assists per game. A four-time NBA champion, James has earned four MVP awards and four Finals MVP awards. He has been selected to 20 All-Star games and was named Rookie of the Year in 2004. In December 2024, he surpassed Kareem Abdul-Jabbar's record for the most career minutes played, reaching 57,447 minutes on the court. James is also the NBA's all-time leading scorer, amassing over 41,000 points. His versatility, longevity, and consistent excellence have solidified his legacy as a transformative figure in basketball history.

2. Larry Bird

Larry Bird had an incredible 13-season career with the Boston Celtics (1979–1992), establishing himself as one of the greatest players in NBA history. He averaged 24.3 points, 10.0 rebounds, and 6.3 assists per game, showcasing his all-around brilliance. Bird was a 3× NBA Champion (1981, 1984, 1986) and won 3 consecutive MVP awards (1984–1986), a rare feat. Known for his elite shooting, high basketball IQ, and clutch performances, he shot 49.6% from the field and 37.6% from three-point range A 12-time All-Star and 10-time All-NBA selection, Bird was also a strong defender, earning 3 All-Defensive Team honors. His legendary battles with Magic Johnson and his fierce competitiveness defined the 1980s NBA era. Bird retired in 1992 due to injuries but remains one of the most respected and influential players in basketball history.

3. Kevin Durant

Kevin Durant, renowned for his scoring prowess and versatility, has had an illustrious NBA career spanning 17 seasons with teams including the Seattle SuperSonics/Oklahoma City Thunder, Golden State Warriors, Brooklyn Nets, and Phoenix Suns. As of February 2025, he has played in 1,094 regular-season games, averaging 27.3 points, 7.0 rebounds, and 4.4 assists per game. Durant has been selected to 14 All-Star games and has earned numerous accolades, including the Rookie of the Year award, one MVP award, two Finals MVP awards, and two NBA championships. He is also approaching the 30,000 career points milestone, underscoring his consistent offensive excellence. Known for his efficiency, Durant boasts a career shooting percentage of 50.1% from the field, 38.7% from three-point range, and 88.4% from the free-throw line. His combination of size, skill, and scoring ability has solidified his status as one of the premier players in NBA history.

4. Scottie Pippen

Scottie Pippen, one of the greatest all-around players in NBA history, played 17 seasons in the league, primarily with the Chicago Bulls alongside Michael Jordan. He averaged 16.1 points, 6.4 rebounds, and 5.2 assists per game over his career, excelling as both a scorer and playmaker. A six-time NBA champion, Pippen was a seven-time All-Star and made 10 NBA All-Defensive Teams, showcasing his elite defensive skills. He was instrumental in the Bulls’ dominance during the 1990s, contributing on both ends of the floor. Pippen also played for the Houston Rockets and Portland Trail Blazers before briefly returning to Chicago. He was inducted into the Basketball Hall of Fame in 2010. Widely regarded as one of the best two-way players ever, Pippen’s versatility helped redefine the role of a forward in the modern NBA.

5. Julius Erving

Julius Erving, also known as "Dr. J," was one of the most electrifying players in basketball history, excelling in both the ABA and NBA. Over his combined 16-season career, he averaged 24.2 points, 8.5 rebounds, and 4.2 assists per game. In the ABA, he was a two-time scoring champion and won two championships before transitioning to the NBA, where he led the Philadelphia 76ers to an NBA title in 1983. Erving was a 16-time All-Star (5 in the ABA, 11 in the NBA) and won the NBA MVP award in 1981. Known for his high-flying dunks and smooth athleticism, he helped revolutionize the small forward position. Dr. J finished his career with 30,026 total points (ABA & NBA combined), ranking among the all-time greats. His influence extended beyond stats, as he was a key figure in popularizing modern above-the-rim play.
